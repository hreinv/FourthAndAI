Why Your Model Has to Show Its Work

A prediction a coach can't interrogate is a prediction a coach won't use.

By [Author Name] · Popularizing [Scholarly Article 2] · 4 min read

Next Gen Stat

A model can be right and still be useless. If it tells a coordinator to expect a blitz but can't say why, the coordinator is left trusting a black box on fourth down — and most coaches won't.

This research takes the prediction problem and adds a harder requirement: the system has to explain its reasoning in terms a football mind already uses — alignment, leverage, tendency — fast enough to act on.

Coach's Note

"Trust isn't earned by being right. It's earned by showing your work."

The payoff is adoption: an explainable call is one a staff can argue with, override, or believe. The authors walk through how they got there in the original paper.
