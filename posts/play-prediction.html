Can a Machine Call the Play Before the Snap?

Inside the research teaching algorithms to read an offense as fast as a coordinator can.

By Hreinson Vanover · Popularizing [Scholarly Article 1] · 4 min read

Next Gen Stat
73.2%
Pre-snap play-type accuracy reported by the model

Every snap starts with a question the defense has milliseconds to answer: run or pass? A team of researchers wondered whether a model could answer it too — not after the game in a film session, but in the seconds before the ball is snapped.

They trained on years of player-tracking data: where everyone lined up, who went in motion, the down and distance. The interesting result wasn't just that it worked — it's how fast it had to work to be useful at all.

Coach's Note

"A prediction that arrives after the snap is just a very confident replay."

That constraint — real time or nothing — is what separates this work from the analytics you already see on broadcasts. The full methodology is laid out in the original paper.
