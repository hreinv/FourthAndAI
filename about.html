About Fourth & AI

Where the research on machine learning meets the line of scrimmage — in plain English.

Fourth & AI is a popularization project: it takes two scholarly articles on real-time, explainable artificial intelligence in American football and rewrites their findings for fans who already live in the analytics layer — the Next Gen Stats checkers, the PFF-podcast listeners — but who don't read journals for fun.

The goal isn't to dumb the research down. It's to keep the rigor and lose the jargon: same ideas, told the way a good analytics column would tell them.

Why this topic

For two seasons I lined up as a Division I defensive lineman, and every snap started the same way: read the formation, the splits, the back's depth — decide run or pass before the ball moved. I was running a model in my head, trained on film and reps. Now I study the machine version of that read. As a computer science and applied mathematics student concentrating in AI/ML, the question these papers ask — can a system make that pre-snap call in real time, and explain it the way a coordinator would? — sits exactly on the line between the two things I know best. This topic isn't abstract to me. I've been the model.

About the author

Hreinson Vanover is a Computer Science and Applied Mathematics double major at the University of San Diego (B.S. expected 2026), concentrating in AI/ML, and a former Division I defensive lineman. He has built AI/ML perception systems as an undergraduate research assistant and works as a software engineering intern on agentic AI workflows. Fourth & AI grew out of a course project on translating research for a general audience — and a long-running interest in where football and machine learning meet. Reach out at hvanover@sandiego.edu.
